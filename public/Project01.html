Remind

A mobile app to help you manage your emotional spendings

Introduction

A mobile app designed to assist in managing emotional spending. It offers a secure ecosystem for tracking, reminders, and sharing, providing personalized tips to help you stay on track with your everyday expenses.

Click for Prototype

Project Context

There has been a surge in discussions about emotional spending lately, with conversations taking place on social media platforms and even documentaries on OTT platforms focusing on promoting minimalism and rational purchasing. In my thesis, I aim to delve into this specific user segment and explore the underlying reasons behind their behavior.

I will investigate their challenges, identify their needs, and propose strategies and solutions to address them effectively.

Process

Followed the double diamond design process aligned with the principles of HCDI. By empathizing with users and delving into the underlying causes of their problems, I gained valuable insights. Throughout the process, I prioritized user input and brainstormed potential solutions. Eventually, I created a detailed prototype and conducted usability tests and pretotype experiments to gather feedback and refine the solution.

Research Methods: User Surveys and Interviews, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Mapping, Root Cause Analysis, Persona Creation, Ideation Workshops, Usability Testing, Pretotype Testing.

Design Methods: Scoping (MOSCOW), User Flows, Information Architecture, Wireframes, Mood Boards, Style Guide, High-Fidelity Interactive prototype.
